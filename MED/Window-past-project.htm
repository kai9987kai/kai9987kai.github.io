Window into the Past

01 · Design brief

Authentic medieval atmosphere, presented clearly.

The original Word-exported layout has been reorganised into focused web sections so the project reads more like a polished portfolio case study.

Project intention

My finalised idea is to produce a dining hall based on a range of reference images of halls from the time period. The plan is to add enough detail to create an authentic look, so stained glass windows and smaller historical details are important.

The scene is historically informative because it shows representations of historical furniture and utensils based on references. The windows use photos of stained glass from real historical buildings as textures.

  • Use candles and chandeliers to create a medieval atmosphere.
  • Use worn wood, wall, and window textures to make the room feel believable.
  • Show what a major hall may have looked like during functions and events.

06 · Research and influence

Media inspiration connected back to project decisions.

The essay content has been split into readable cards, so it supports the project instead of feeling like a separate document.

Assassin's Creed Valhalla

One example of media set in this time period is the game Assassin's Creed Valhalla. It educates players by using historical events to tell a story. Although the buildings are not always 100 percent accurate, historical maps and local educational experiences helped inform the world.

Knightfall

Another example is Knightfall, which tells the story of the Knights Templar. The show presents characters based on real people and uses clothing inspired by what the Templar army would have worn.

07 · Evaluation

What worked, and what could be pushed further.

The evaluation now reads like a final reflection for a portfolio or project submission.

What went well

The project went well in terms of accuracy and creating the assets I planned. The atmosphere created by the candles and the full room helped the project come together effectively.

The dining hall concept fits the “window into the past” theme because it aims to show what a historical hall may have looked like.

Next level improvements

To advance the project further, I would push the quality of the models and look for smaller surface details, such as wood grain and higher-resolution texture variation, to make the environment more believable.

  • Add close-up prop renders for the strongest assets.
  • Show wireframes or process screenshots beside final renders.
  • Add texture breakdowns for stained glass, wood, stone, and candlelight.
