Help / how to use AR Fire Lab V8.6

Use this panel as a built-in guide for people trying the demo for the first time. It explains setup, scanning, controls, recording, presets, and common fixes without needing a separate README.

Quick start
1. Open it safely
  1. Host this HTML file on HTTPS or run it from localhost.
  2. Open it on a phone or laptop with a camera.
  3. Allow camera access when the browser asks.
2. Show the Hiro marker
  1. Press Hiro to open the marker image.
  2. Print it or show it on a second screen.
  3. Keep all four corners visible inside the scanner.
  4. Use Scan Boost when the camera is blurry, low resolution, shaky, or struggling in dim light.
3. Control the fire
  • Burst creates an instant flare and shockwave.
  • Mode changes the colour world: ember, plasma, aurora, quantum, cryoflame, voidfire, and more.
  • FX cycles extra layered effects such as cinder rain, plasma arcs, core lens, storm forge, and spectral overload.
  • FX Depth controls how intense the extra effects are.
  • Drag the floating pad to change wind and heat quickly.
4. Make it stable
  • Use Optimize if FPS drops or the phone gets hot.
  • Leave Guardian and Auto Quality on for public demos.
  • Use Focus Marker on supported browsers when the marker looks blurry.
  • Use Camera to switch between front and rear cameras.
5. Capture and share
  • Snapshot exports a PNG where the browser allows canvas capture.
  • Record saves a short WebM/MP4-style recording when MediaRecorder is available.
  • Save stores the current setup locally.
  • Share writes preset JSON into this panel so someone else can paste it into their copy.
  • Export Log saves diagnostics for debugging camera, FPS, battery, and tracking issues.
Troubleshooting
  • No camera: check browser permission, use HTTPS/localhost, then press Camera.
  • No marker lock: reduce glare, flatten the marker, and move the camera back until all corners are visible.
  • Lag: lower Particle Budget, Haze, FX Depth, or press Optimize.
  • Black screen after switching camera: reload once; the app preserves your last preset when possible.
What V8.5 innovates and adds

V8.5 turns the project into a guided adaptive marker/XR fire engine. It keeps the reliable Hiro-marker base, then adds a user help system, effect packs, richer procedural layers, session health scoring, wake-lock support, focus/exposure assist, safer fallbacks, and a cleaner path toward markerless WebXR hit-test placement.

Novel upgrade ideas built into the architecture

  • Guided help layer: the app now teaches setup, scanning, controls, recording, preset sharing, and troubleshooting inside the live UI.
  • Effect packs: a new FX control activates layered cinder rain, plasma arcs, core lensing, storm-style overload, or a full spectral stack.
  • Reactive FX depth: one slider scales the extra procedural layers so demos can move between subtle and dramatic without editing code.
  • New flame worlds: cryoflame, spectral, and voidfire extend the colour/mood system beyond fire, plasma, ion, and molten looks.
  • Sensor-reactive fire: optional tilt wind maps phone orientation into flame drift.
  • Thermal atmosphere: animated heat-haze plane and pulsing ground rings sell the illusion without heavy models.
  • Performance governor: particle budget rises or falls based on measured FPS and marker stability.
  • Creative capture: snapshot, short WebM recording when supported, exportable presets, and shareable JSON.
  • Smart director: automatically shapes heat, wind, mode, reactivity, and particle density when the marker is stable.
  • Environmental presets: Calm, Storm, Zero-G, Cinematic, Ion Fog, Afterburner, Cryo Drift, and Void Bloom quickly remix the flame physics.
  • WebXR-ready: detects immersive AR support so a future markerless hit-test scene can be added cleanly.
  • Guardian mode: responds to marker loss, low FPS, low battery, and thermal load by reducing visual pressure before the demo collapses.
  • Camera health loop: watches the live webcam track, reports resolution/facing mode, and provides a fast restart path if the feed stalls.
  • Session health model: blends rolling FPS, marker stability, battery state, camera state, and error count into one practical health score.
