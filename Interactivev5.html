Simulation Information

This version preserves the original agents, threats, food, tasks, obstacles, controls, day/night cycle, learning memory, data augmentation, stats, and log panel. It upgrades them into a more experimental artificial-life lab.

  • Intrinsic motivation: agents receive curiosity reward for visiting novel cells and completing new task types.
  • Quality-diversity archive: successful genomes are stored by behavioural niche, so the population does not collapse into one “best” strategy.
  • Cellular field: food, agents, and threats continuously write nutrient, trail, and danger signals into a local grid that diffuses and decays.
  • Open-ended events: bloom, drought, storm, migration, and predator-pulse events reshape the environment during the run.
  • Real energy economy: energy decay now affects movement, health, reproduction, task completion, and death.
  • Comparison merge: adds rotate90/rotate180 augmentation, true fullscreen-style simulation behaviour, capped logs, live step-cost timing, and a clearer warm-start/pre-training status.
  • Lab workflow: brush tools, inspect mode, elite seeding, JSON export/import, theater view, and minimal HUD make experiments easier to run and compare.
  • Research director: watches population, novelty, archive coverage, and food pressure, then injects curriculum tasks instead of random-only events.
  • Red Queen pressure: threats slowly adapt when agents dominate, while struggling populations trigger gentler rescue conditions.
  • Signal communication + ghost probes: agents leave lightweight social traces and the lab samples counterfactual futures to nudge task placement.
  • v5 research layer: adds symbiotic guild learning, epigenetic plasticity, anomaly/collapse guard, adaptive habitat engineering, and micro-experiment referee notes.

Research-Inspired Experiment Notes

What to watch: a healthy world should show shifting niches, not just rising population. If one strategy dominates, increase mutation or turn on more events.

  • Blue/cyan agents tend to be fast foragers.
  • Purple/pink agents usually have stronger curiosity or risk traits.
  • Yellow tasks become curriculum landmarks: they reward agents for exploring beyond food chasing.
  • Red fields show remembered danger. Green fields show nutrients. Purple/blue fields show social trails.

Invented addition: “regenerative pressure” links scarcity to field fertility. If agents over-consume without exploration, the map becomes less fertile; diverse movement helps new food patches appear.

Borrowed from the comparison version: minimal fullscreen overlay thinking, direct performance telemetry, capped/autoscrolling logs, resize-first canvas behaviour, and simple always-visible controls. Kept from our version: QD archive, local cellular fields, evolution, tasks, particles, scenarios, localStorage, snapshot, JSON tools, theater mode, and richer controls.

New v4 invention: “counterfactual curriculum pressure” runs cheap ghost probes around the world and turns promising areas into experimental task clusters, so the world teaches agents instead of only reacting to them.

New v5 invention: “closed-loop experiment referee” watches collapse, stagnation, over-dominance, and under-exploration. It writes short notes to the event log, launches small test tasks, restores habitats, and nudges agents through lifetime plasticity instead of only mutation.

Feature audit: v5 preserves every major chain feature: original entities and controls, day/night, threats/resources/tasks/obstacles, logs/stats/performance, learning and augmentation, field ecology, evolution, QD archive, localStorage, scenarios, brush/inspect tools, JSON import/export, snapshot download, theater/minimal HUD, research director, Red Queen, social learning, signals, and ghost probes.
