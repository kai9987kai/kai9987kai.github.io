Select an organism in the habitat to inspect its inherited traits, energy, ancestry, and local thermal match.

Energy creates tradeoffs

Speed, body size, sensing, and heat mismatch all carry energetic costs. Survival and reproduction emerge from resource capture rather than a fixed fitness score.

Selection varies in space and time

A cool-to-warm habitat gradient, seasons, and droughts create changing local niches. Thermal preference is inherited, allowing adaptation to be observed rather than scripted.

Research-informed, not predictive

This is an exploratory model, not a forecast. Its mechanisms draw on recent work in bioenergetics, local adaptation, and mutation in finite populations.
