Space Tribology & Materials

35 %
Autocatalytic PFPE Cleavage: High boundary forces expose fresh iron, creating FeF₃ (a strong Lewis acid) which chemically unzips PFPE polymer chains into outgassing volatiles. X-1P additive passivates the active sites to dramatically slow this.
MoS₂ Solid Film: Boundary sliding mechanically aligns basal planes for super-low friction (Run-In). However, pre-launch humidity or operational temperatures above 77°C causes severe oxidation into abrasive MoO₃.

Gear Dimensions & Stiffness

—
Backlash shifts dynamically with steel-gear wear and thermal expansion differences. Under high operational heat, low-CTE housings (Titanium) expand slower than steel gears, potentially squeezing backlash to zero resulting in a physical Thermal Lock.

Dynamic Drives & Inertias

Dynamic Transmission Error (DTE) tracks instantaneous angular position inaccuracies caused by tooth deflection, wear profile deviations, and backlash slap. The PID controller mimics closed-loop space mechanism dynamics. Archard wear physics are still utilized. (ASME Review)
