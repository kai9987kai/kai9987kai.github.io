Interpretation logic

Pneumonia weighting increases with hypoxia, pleuritic pain, focal imaging change, high CRP/PCT, productive sputum, and bacterial pathogen evidence. Viral bronchitis weighting rises with wheeze, cough, normal oxygenation, low PCT, viral pathogen evidence, and non-focal imaging.

      Chest Infection (Bronchitis) Symptoms

      • Persistent Cough: May be dry or productive with clear, yellow, or green mucus.
      • Low-Grade Fever: Mild temperature increase, usually not exceeding 38°C (100.4°F).
      • Chest Discomfort: Mild tightness or soreness.
      • Wheezing: Especially during exertion.
      • Sore Throat/Runny Nose: Common in viral infections.
      • Fatigue: General tiredness and malaise.

      Pneumonia Symptoms

      • High Fever: Often above 38°C (100.4°F) and may reach up to 40°C (104°F).
      • Shaking Chills: Intense episodes of shivering.
      • Severe, Productive Cough: With yellow, green, or blood-tinged sputum.
      • Pleuritic Chest Pain: Sharp, stabbing pain worsening with deep breaths or coughing.
      • Marked Shortness of Breath: Difficulty breathing even at rest.
      • Confusion: Particularly in older adults.
      • Gastrointestinal Symptoms: Loss of appetite, nausea, or vomiting.
      • Cyanosis: Bluish discoloration in severe cases.

      Side-by-Side Comparison

      Symptom Chest Infection (Bronchitis) Pneumonia
      Cough Persistent; may be dry or produce mucus. Severe; productive with discolored or blood-tinged sputum.
      Fever Low-grade, mild increase. High-grade, often exceeding 38°C (100.4°F).
      Chest Pain Mild discomfort or tightness. Sharp, pleuritic pain.
      Breathing Difficulty Occasional wheezing. Marked shortness of breath.
      Systemic Symptoms Fatigue, sore throat, runny nose. Confusion, loss of appetite, nausea, cyanosis.

      Additional Details

      A weighted approach using symptom severity and risk factors improves diagnostic accuracy. Advanced methods such as cough sound analysis, simulated lung sound evaluation, wearable data, and configurable weighting further refine the assessment.
